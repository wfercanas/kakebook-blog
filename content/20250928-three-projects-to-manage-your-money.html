Three Projects for Managing Your Money

It's hard to manage something that cannot be seen. You need to see your money and how you use it before you can get better at managing it.

I've tried using a single project/book to keep track of all my expenses, but I've found myself trapped many times in so many details that I eventually stopped keeping track of my money.

Now I think it would be better to manage money using three separate projects. These projects are:

  1. Personal Project
  2. Investment Project
  3. Home Project

Projects

Let's see their core characteristics:

Personal Project

  1. This project tracks personal expenses only.
  2. Has some categories in which you tend to spend money. Mark each expense under a single category.
  3. Start each month by adding some money to the project. The rest of the month is just about registering how you used it.
  4. If you need more money during the month it's okay. Add more if you have it available.
  5. Dedicate 10-15 minutes daily to keep the records up to date.
  6. Don't overcomplicate it by recording entries with too much detail or complex figures.

Investment Project

  1. This project exists only to handle your investments.
  2. This project usually doesn't have too many movements.
  3. Add money on a regular basis, invest it right away, and let time and compound interest do their magic.
  4. Create categories if you have more than one kind of investment.
  5. Dedicate 5-10 minutes monthly to keep it up to date
  6. Whenever you do an investment, record it right away.

Home Project

  1. Expenses here tend to be regular, since they typically are related to your bills.
  2. However, there may be some unexpected situations that are important to resolve and record.
  3. Record each expense right away.
  4. Group your expenses according to the day you pay them. Try to create the minimal amount of groups. It will reduce the days you must register movements.
  5. Dedicate 5-10 minutes each month to check that these expenses look like the ones from last month.

Budget

If you have all your money in cash (rare nowadays), you can split the money into three. Assign some of it to each project.

If you have your money in a bank account, try to split it into three pockets. One pocket for each project.

You can also have three different bank accounts, one per project. Although this can improve the control of your money, keep in mind that it may carry some costs.

Feedback

The first month is just about starting. Take a guess at the budget and start.

The important part comes from month two onwards. Check how well you managed your money last month and make corrections.

The point here is to make improvements, whether they are big or small ones. It doesn't matter as long as you keep learning.

Try new things and see their results.

Try not to invest less than 10% of your income. Have it as your baseline.

Enjoy!
